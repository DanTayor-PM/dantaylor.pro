Case Study 02

Cross-Product Dependency Management

…

Challenge

An ambitious product roadmap required work to flow seamlessly across multiple product teams, but no system existed to manage cross-product dependencies. Handovers risked bottlenecks and miscommunication, limiting the organisation’s ability to deliver truly modular, integrated solutions.

→

Approach

Designed and implemented a comprehensive dependency management system capable of handling complex cross-product chains. Created visual “battle plan” diagrams that mapped every touchpoint, deadline, and handover — giving teams full visibility of their role in the wider delivery picture.

✓

Outcome

Transformed how the organisation approached development. Teams understood not just their tasks, but their impact. The visual diagrams became essential communication tools, helping stakeholders across the business appreciate the complexity and importance of following the plan — a critical step towards product-led growth.

Case Study 04

Resource Allocation & Budget Clarity

…

Challenge

Organic growth had created complexity in resource allocation. Timesheet systems and capacity files had evolved independently across teams, making it difficult to determine true headcount ownership. Initial analysis suggested the product function was 10% over budget.

→

Approach

Conducted a comprehensive resource audit, cataloguing every team member and aligning them to their correct cost centres. Built a unified allocation model that provided leadership with an accurate, real-time view of where every resource sat and what they were working on.

✓

Outcome

Proved the product function was operating within budget — the apparent overspend was an allocation issue, not a cost issue. This clarity directly protected roles that would otherwise have been at risk, while giving executives confidence in their financial reporting.
